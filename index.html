Where nature, wellness, culture and hospitality are immersed within a restored Illawarra landscape.

Site Location

Site Location

The former Huntley and Avondale Colliery site located 15 mins south west of Wollongong and 1hr 25mins from the Sydney CBD.

The Avondale Site is 317 hectares in area, approximately 2.8km north/south and 1.4km east/west. The site has a 450m fall from west to east and has views from Port Kembla to Shell Harbour over Lake Illawarra.

1946 — 1989 · Huntley Colliery

History

The site has a rich history of agricultural use before becoming home to the NSW Government-owned Huntley Colliery, which operated from 1946 to 1989. Originally established as a small hand-worked mine, the colliery expanded significantly following the discovery of the Wongawilli seam in 1952, becoming one of Australia’s most productive coal mines and the first in Australia to exceed one million tonnes of annual production by 1966. At its peak, the mine employed almost 500 people and played an important role in the Illawarra’s industrial development.

These mining operations significantly altered parts of the landscape, creating access roads, hardstand areas and modified landforms that remain evident today. Following the closure of the mine, the site transitioned primarily to grazing land, with the former mining areas largely underutilised. The proposed redevelopment provides an opportunity to positively transform these previously disturbed lands through environmental rehabilitation, landscape restoration and adaptive re-use, creating a new destination that acknowledges the site’s agricultural and industrial heritage while delivering lasting environmental, social and economic benefits.

The Pathway Ahead

Planning Process

Hover or focus a stage to read more

  1. Project Architect / Lead Designer Established

    Award winning architectural firm Turner Studio will be leading the design process.

  2. Visioning and Concept Design Work

    The development of a vision and design concept of the Avondale Lodge and Spa.

  3. Request for SEAR’s

    Application to the NSW state government requesting the Secretary’s Environmental Assessment Requirements (SEARs). The SEARs sets out all the requirements to be met for lodging a State Significant Development application to the NSW Government.

  4. Concept Refinement

    Taking on board community & stakeholder feedback, results of site investigations and feedback/advice from SDRP, the concept designs are further developed and refined.

  5. We Are Here

    This is the current stage of the planning process.

  6. Design Development

    The refined concept is progressed into detailed architectural and landscape design, resolving the proposal ahead of formal application.

  7. Lodge SSD Application

    Make formal application to the state government lodging all project design documentation, site assessments and technical reports developed as part of the proposal.

  8. Finalise Proposal

    Making all final considerations of feedback received from the community & stakeholders, SDRP, local Council, traditional custodians & representatives, & site specific studies and assessments, all required designs and reports shall be finalised and compiled ready for submission to the state government.

  9. Public Exhibition

    SSD application is publicly exhibited for a minimum period of 28 days. Surrounding residents are notified by the department in writing. The application is also made available through the NSW government ‘Major Projects’ web site. You can have your say on the proposed development by making submissions during the public exhibition period.

  10. Assessment & Recommendation

    The department assesses the application and makes a recommendation set out in an environmental assessment report. The recommendation, including either conditions of consent or reasons for refusal, is referred to the minister or delegate for a final determination.

  11. Final Determination

    The minister makes a final determination to approve or refuse the application.

The Project

The project will deliver authentic and immersive guest experiences with a focus on storytelling, pre and post European history, including former mining operations, and the unique Illawarra Landscape.

The project delivers substantial and much-needed environmental protection and restoration works, celebrating the site’s natural values through an interconnected network of enhanced visitor experiences, including walking trails, boardwalks, lookouts, viewing decks and gathering spaces that foster meaningful engagement with the regenerated landscape.

A lasting contribution

Benefits

Rehabilitation and beneficial re-use of a former mining site and industrial heritage assets.

Significant environmental restoration and protection outcomes on previously disturbed land, with no native forest clearing.

Creation of a unique, internationally recognised eco-tourism and wellness destination for the Illawarra.

Increased tourism, economic activity and investment aligned with NSW visitor economy objectives.

Employment, training and local business opportunities that support regional prosperity.

Delivery of much-needed high-end immersive eco-tourism accommodation in regional NSW.

Enhanced appreciation and activation of the Illawarra escarpment landscape and surrounding visitor experiences.

Design Team

The design team is led by Nick Turner, whose 25+ years of experience is underpinned by a passion for rehabilitating and reimagining sites through adaptive reuse.

His approach balances the preservation of a place’s history with contemporary design, creating hospitality destinations that strengthen community connections while celebrating local character. By finding the right balance between heritage and innovation, Nick delivers memorable hotel experiences that inspire people to visit, gather, and return.
